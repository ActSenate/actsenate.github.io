Funding

By far the most important issue the Senate addresses is funding.

Every year the Senate has to cope with a staggering number of funding requests, despite having very limited funds to disburse. Overspending is frequently an issue. Fortunately, the funding reform that was implemented earlier this year should begin to solve some of Senate’s budgetary issues – but it has to be implemented successfully in order to work. We promise to approach Senate with the intention of enforcing fiscal responsibility and fiscal fairness. Among our proposed initiatives:

Mandatory weekly budgeting for Senate, with specific spending targets

Working with other funding groups on campus – the Stanford Fund, Stanford Student Enterprises, etc – to put out a packet recommending alternative funding for groups who are denied funds

Encouraging fiscal responsibility instead of hiking up the student activities fee

Outreach

Stanford would be nothing without its student groups and organizations...

... and Stanford is fortunate to have so many diverse communities on campus. Given that the Senate’s decisions have such a huge impact on student life at Stanford, it is crucial that the Senate have an accurate picture of every community’s needs. The Senate cannot afford to make decisions blindly. That’s why we support required weekly outreach for every Senator – that means organizing private meetings with student groups, attending community events as ambassadors of the ASSU, and even tabling in White Plaza on a regular basis to answer questions and speak with concerned students.

Accountability & Transparency

Let’s face it: a lot of students have a very limited picture of what exactly the Senate is...

... and what the ASSU in general, does here at Stanford. That’s a problem – the Senate cannot be held accountable for good decision-making if nobody knows what decisions they’re making in the first place. We intend to implement reforms that make the Senate much more transparent for Stanford students. Among our plans:

Building a revamped website devoted entirely to the Senate, with weekly updates on Senate activities

Forming a third party organization to provide weekly summaries of Senate meetings – because the full minutes are full of extraneous information and annoying to read through

Tabling in White Plaza – which we mentioned under the “Outreach” pillar of our platform. Tabling not only forces the Senate to deal directly with the study body as their representatives, but it also keeps the Senate accountable. If the Senate ever makes a decision that misrepresents the student body, we can be sure the Senators manning the table will hear about it
